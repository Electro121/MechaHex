About our Project

Natural disasters like earthquakes often leave behind unstable structures and debris, making search and rescue operations difficult for first responders. To address this challenge, we have developed a six-legged hexapod spider robot equipped with an ESP32-CAM module, a smoke sensor, and a temperature sensor. Designed to navigate through the rubble of destroyed buildings, this robot aims to assist in disaster zones by providing real-time data and visuals from areas that are otherwise inaccessible or too dangerous for humans.

Features

  • 20 Servo Motors for Leg and Camera Movement
  • Live Video Streaming
  • Environmental Sensors (DHT11 & MQ2)

Project Journey

The robot is constructed using high-torque servomotors that articulate its six legs, enabling complex movements like climbing over obstacles and adjusting its stance for stability. The ESP32- CAM module serves dual purposes: it streams live video and handles wireless communication. Sensors are interfaced using efficient protocols to ensure real-time data transmission without latency. We have programmed custom algorithms for movement control and sensor data processing, optimizing the robot's performance in challenging environments. Emphasis was placed on modularity and robustness to withstand the harsh conditions of disaster zones.
